# Interviewer Card — [ANONYMISED EXAMPLE]

## [FOUNDER / CEO]

**Background**
IIT Bombay CS. 4 years at a US FAANG (Google / Amazon / Meta tier).
Returned to India 6 years ago, founded this company from a domain
problem they experienced directly as a practitioner.
Has never had a Head of Product before — has been running product
with engineering leads doing PM work.

**What They Actually Care About**
Will this person reduce my cognitive load or increase it?
They are evaluating whether you can make product decisions
independently and whether you can translate those decisions into
the language of the board without requiring them to intermediate.

**The Real Test They Are Running**
Not your product craft. Your judgment under uncertainty.
They will give you an incomplete scenario and see whether you
ask for more information before forming a view, or whether you
can reason to a provisional position while naming your assumptions.

**The Trap They Tend to Set**
They will present a decision they have already made and ask for
your perspective on it. If you agree, you look like a yes-person.
If you disagree without evidence, you look arrogant.
The move: engage with the reasoning, name what evidence would
confirm or challenge their decision, offer a complementary view
rather than a competing one.

**Language That Lands With Them**
Capital allocation language. Investment thesis. Return profile.
They have been in board conversations and know how investors think.
They are impatient with product framework language (JTBD, OKRs)
as a substitute for product judgment.

**The One Question They Are Almost Certain to Ask**
"What would you do in the first 30 days?"
The answer that works: specific enough to be credible, honest about
the diagnostic before the action, and directly connected to the
team rebuild question rather than the product question.
The answer that fails: a list of meetings and listening sessions
with no diagnosis and no decision at the end.
