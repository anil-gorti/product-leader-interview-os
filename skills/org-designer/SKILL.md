# Skill: Org Designer

## Trigger
"How do I think about the team I am inheriting"
"What does a PM capability assessment look like"
"How do I approach the first 30 days with the team"
"What is my hiring plan"

## The Senior Product Leader Team Question
At Director level and above, the interview question is rarely
"what would you build?" It is "what would you do with the team
you are inheriting?" Most candidates are not prepared for this.

## The Capability Assessment Framework
Evaluate each PM on six dimensions:

1. Problem clarity — can they write a problem statement that
   changes direction rather than confirms it?
2. Customer proximity — are they speaking to customers weekly
   and is it changing what they build?
3. Platform thinking — do they think in primitives or features?
4. Commercial judgment — can they trace a product decision
   to a commercial metric?
5. Upmarket buyer instinct — have they built for a buyer
   (procurement, CFO, GC) rather than just a user?
6. Influence without authority — do they hold positions
   under stakeholder pressure?

## The Three Category Output
Category 1: Invest and accelerate — written development plan by day 45
Category 2: Honest conversation — is the capability gap closeable
   in the company's timeline? Have this conversation by day 45.
Category 3: The hire — what profile does not exist on the team
   and needs to be brought in?

## The Bodies Diagnostic
The tendency to throw headcount at problems that are product design failures.
The PM team that adds a coordination meeting when spec quality should have
prevented the need. The CS team that adds a support engineer when the error
message should have prevented the ticket.
Bodies replace design. Design prevents bodies.
Name this pattern explicitly when discussing team structure.

## Output Format
Assessment card per PM: current strength / key gap / decision by day 60
Hiring plan: profile / non-negotiable bar / source / timeline
The one thing you will not change about the team structure immediately
   and why (demonstrates listening before leading)
