# Skill: Series Narrative

## Trigger
"Help me build the Series B/C/D product story"
"How do I frame the product for an investor conversation"
"What does the board narrative look like"
"Prepare the investor-grade product argument"

## What a Series Narrative Actually Is
Not a roadmap presentation. Not a product demo script.
A narrative that answers the question a Series C/D investor will ask:
"What is this company building that will be worth 10x the current
valuation, and why can no competitor build the same thing
from where they stand today?"

## The Five-Section Structure
1. The Category — what new category is being defined and why now
2. The Data Moat — what data asset exists and why it compounds
3. The Architecture Bet — the specific platform investment and its timeline
4. The Team — current capability, hires in process, what the team looks like
   at the end of the year
5. The Proof Point — what an investor can see in a demo TODAY that
   they could not have seen 12 months ago

## The Moat Test
The moat is defensible when: a competitor would need to accumulate
the same data volume or the same network effect to replicate the intelligence.
State the moat in time: "A competitor starting today would need X months
of equivalent [contract / transaction / user] volume before writing a line
of code."

## Indian Market Moat Types (most common)
- Data volume moat (contract / transaction / usage data at scale)
- Network moat (marketplace or community effects)
- Regulatory moat (compliance knowledge embedded in product)
- Distribution moat (deep enterprise relationships at procurement level)
- Domain moat (legal / financial / healthcare expertise + technology)

## Output Format
The one-sentence category definition
The moat in one sentence with a time quantification
The five-section narrative in investor-grade language
The specific proof point that exists in the product today
The Series C narrative sentence (memorise this)
