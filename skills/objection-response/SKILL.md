# Skill: Objection Response

## Trigger
"How do I answer if they say X"
"Practice the [objection] objection"
"What if they push back on Y"
"Run the hardest version of [concern]"

## The Senior Objection Architecture
A strong response to a C-suite objection has three moves:

1. Concede the legitimate part — never dodge what is genuinely true
2. Reframe with specific evidence — not general assertions
3. Close with conviction — the candidate who closes with a hedge
   confirms the concern rather than resolving it

## The Objections to Load at Senior Level

From knowledge/india-context/c-suite-objections.md:
- "You have not operated at this scale before"
- "The team you would inherit is already strong"
- "Why are you available?"
- "You spent X years outside India"
- "What if the team resists your direction?"
- "You are overqualified"
- "Why leave large company for a startup?"

Plus role-specific objections from knowledge/target-company/interviewers.md

## Process
1. Identify: is this a legitimate concern or a conviction test?
   Legitimate concern = the interviewer has a real risk they are managing
   Conviction test = they want to see if you will fold under pressure
2. For legitimate concerns: concede / reframe / close
3. For conviction tests: hold the position, name the evidence, do not soften

## Output Format
The concession: [what is legitimately true — say it plainly]
The reframe: [specific evidence that changes the frame]
The close: [conviction, not hedge]
Do not say: [the specific phrase that would confirm the concern]
The tell: [how to know if this landed]
