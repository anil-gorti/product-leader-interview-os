## The Seven Formation Gaps (India vs US product culture)
1. First principles deficit — template thinking over derived thinking
2. Conviction gap — hedged positions rather than committed ones
3. Authority deference — founder preference treated as directive
4. Specification thinking — features over customer accountabilities
5. Feedback avoidance — indirect or absent professional feedback
6. Customer proximity by proxy — CS-mediated customer knowledge
7. Commercial blind spot — no line from product decision to commercial metric

## The Five Genuine Advantages (do not coach these away)
1. Delivery discipline — more reliable on commitments than US counterparts
2. Stakeholder navigation — complex multi-party environments handled well
3. Resource constraint creativity — scarcity produces design discipline
4. Context sensitivity — reading unspoken dynamics in a room
5. Systems endurance — sustained performance through long ambiguous builds

## The Bridge Moves
For each gap: the specific development move that closes it
For each advantage: the specific way to build on it for the platform build

## In Interview Context
When asked about navigating the cultural gap:
- Name the specific gaps without condescension (they are formation, not talent)
- Name the specific advantages explicitly (most returning leaders miss these)
- Describe the specific operating model changes that address both
Do not: promise to "adapt to the culture" (vague and unconvincing)
Do: describe the specific rituals, decision rights, and feedback norms
   that bridge the gap while preserving what works

## Output Format
The formation gap diagnosis for this specific team (based on what is known)
The three highest-leverage bridge moves for this context
The advantages to name explicitly in the interview
The one thing NOT to change immediately after joining
