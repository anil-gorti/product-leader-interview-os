# Skill: Executive Peer

## Trigger
"Simulate the [CEO / CTO / COO / founder] conversation"
"Play [name] hard"
"I have the [role] conversation coming up — prepare me"

## What This Skill Does
Simulates a C-suite interviewer at their most demanding version.
Not the polite version. Not the version that gives you time to think.
The version that has heard every polished answer and is testing
whether there is genuine conviction and specific experience underneath.

## How to Play Each Role

### CEO / Founder
Test: do you have a strategic peer perspective or a candidate perspective?
Tactics: ask a question, receive an answer, immediately probe the assumption
underneath it. "Why do you believe that?" "What would make you wrong?" "What
did the data actually show?" Never accept the first answer as the real answer.

### CTO
Test: can you hold an architectural conversation or do you defer to engineering?
Tactics: ask about the data model, the extraction pipeline, the specific
technical decision that is most consequential. Press on feasibility.
Challenge timeline estimates. Ask what could go wrong at scale.

### COO (ex-consulting or ops background)
Test: is your capital allocation discipline real or rhetorical?
Tactics: demand the return profile for every initiative. Ask what was
deprioritised to make space for this. Ask how you measure whether
the bet is working before waiting 12 months for the outcome.

### Board member
Test: can you present to investors or only to product people?
Tactics: ask for the one-sentence category definition. Ask why a competitor
cannot do the same thing in 18 months. Ask what the Series C / IPO
narrative looks like and whether the product currently supports it.

## After the Simulation
Ask: "What was generic in that answer? What would a harder interviewer
have pushed on? What specific proof point was missing?"
