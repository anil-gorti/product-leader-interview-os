# Skill: Story Retrieval

## Trigger
"Which story should I use for X"
"Give me a story about Y"
"How do I answer a question about Z"
"I need a proof point for [topic]"

## Process
1. Load knowledge/_me/proof-points.md — this is the only source of metrics
2. Load knowledge/analogies/ — find the parallel that maps to the question
3. Load memory/interview-sessions/ — check what has landed before
4. Never invent a number. Never estimate. Only cite from proof-points.md.

## The Senior Story Standard
A strong senior product leader story has four components:

1. The bet — what was the specific investment decision you made
   and what alternatives were you choosing between?
2. The evidence — what did the data say before and after?
3. The system insight — what did this decision teach you about
   how product, engineering, and commercial outcomes connect?
4. The transfer — how does this specific experience apply to
   what this company needs right now?

A story that is missing the transfer is a career story.
A story that has the transfer is a candidate argument.

## Output Format
Story: [which one, why this is the right choice for this question]
The anchor metric: [exact number from proof-points.md]
The bet framing: [investment decision language, not feature language]
The transfer: [explicit connection to the target company context]
One-line version: [for time-constrained delivery]
Watch for: [the overuse risk or the trap this story contains]
