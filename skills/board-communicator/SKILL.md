# Skill: Board Communicator

## Trigger
"How do I write the board-level product narrative"
"Help me prepare the investor update"
"What does the monthly strategy memo look like"
"How do I frame product progress for a board audience"

## The Board Audience Standard
Board members and investors evaluate product updates against three questions:
1. Is the strategic bet still the right bet?
2. Is there evidence the bet is working?
3. Is the risk being managed?

They do not evaluate: features shipped, velocity, team happiness, roadmap detail.

## The Monthly Strategy Memo Format
One page. Five sections that do not change:
1. What changed in the product this month (specific, not listed)
2. What the data says about whether the platform bet is working
3. The hardest decision made this month and the reasoning
4. The one thing most at risk in the next month and the mitigation
5. The Series C/IPO proof point as of this date — what can be seen
   in the product now that could not be seen last month?

## The Uncomfortable Test
If section 4 (what is most at risk) is consistently described as
"manageable" or "on track" — the memo is being written for optics.
The board memo that is honest enough to be uncomfortable is the one
that builds genuine credibility over time.

## In Interview Context
When asked about executive communication style:
- Name the memo format explicitly (it signals discipline and clarity)
- Describe the uncomfortable test (it signals intellectual honesty)
- Give a specific example of a memo that named bad news before
  being asked (the most credible version of the commitment)

## Output Format
The five-section memo template with section guidance
The specific language to use for each section at this company's stage
The one thing this board most needs to hear that they are probably not hearing
