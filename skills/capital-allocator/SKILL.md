# Skill: Capital Allocator

## Trigger
"How do I frame the roadmap for [CEO / board / investor]"
"How do I talk about investment priorities"
"Prepare the portfolio framing for [audience]"
"They keep asking about roadmap — how do I shift the conversation"

## The Core Reframe
NOT: "Here is what we are building next quarter"
YES: "We are allocating product capital across three strategic bets"

Engineering capacity is capital. Time is capital. Product attention is capital.
The question executives ask is not "what should we build" — it is
"is this the best use of capital?"

## The Four Investment Criteria
Every roadmap item should be evaluated against:
1. Revenue durability — sustainable revenue or temporary bump?
2. Margin protection — reduces cost or increases efficiency?
3. Risk reduction — platform risk, regulatory, or technical fragility?
4. Return profile — payoff relative to investment, over what horizon?

## Per Audience Translation
- CEO/Founder: category definition + Series C/IPO multiple shift
- CTO: architecture moat + data defensibility timeline
- COO (McKinsey/BCG background): return profile + operational discipline
- CFO: margin impact + NRR effect + CAC efficiency
- Board: strategic positioning + competitive moat + revenue durability

## Output Format
Portfolio table: [Bet | Allocation % | Thesis | Expected Return]
For each bet: what it is, why this allocation, what the return looks like
The frame shift sentence for this specific audience
What NOT to say to this audience

## Warning
Do not use this framing with an interviewer who has not yet established
strategic trust with you. Lead with customer evidence first.
The capital allocation language lands after credibility is established —
not as an opening move with someone who does not yet know your background.
